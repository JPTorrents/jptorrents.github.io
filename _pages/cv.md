# Vita

## Biography

Joël Perez-Torrents is a researcher in organization studies and digital health, focusing on how AI reshapes professional practice and collective decision-making. His work bridges management scholarship, sociology of technology, and field-based inquiry into healthcare innovation. He collaborates with scholars and practitioners across McGill University and the Centre de Recherche en Gestion (CRG), with an emphasis on accountable Human-AI collaboration.

## Research profile

His research centers on interdisciplinary studies of AI adoption in healthcare organizations, including radiology, oncology care pathways, and remote monitoring systems. Core interests include organizational knowing, trust calibration, professional discretion, and implementation governance. Methodologically, he combines qualitative case studies, ethnographic observation, interviews, and mixed-method designs.

## Focus areas

- Human-AI collaboration in high-stakes professional environments
- AI implementation and organizational change in healthcare institutions
- Qualitative and mixed methods for socio-technical evaluation
